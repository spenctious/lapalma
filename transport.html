Transport

Bus

The bus company is TILP (Transportes Insular La Palma S. Coop.). The service is very useful for walkers allowing a walking holiday to be undertaken without hiring a car. Even those with transport will find the service opens up a number of linear routes that would otherwise be impractical.

Timetables

Printed timetables can sometimes be got from the tourist information centres. The TILP website is not as good as it once was. You can still download a PDF of the main timetable and any special timetables (for holiday periods for example) from the TILP website.

Routes and main services

When using buses for linear walks it is best to drive to the finish and take the bus to the start as you can be reasonably sure of your timings that way wheras if you miss the bus at the end you may have a long wait until the next one.

Taxis

Taxis on La Palma are reasonably priced and are a useful option for walkers. A few companies will let you book online for bigger trips but mostly booking is over the phone. Most companies will be sufficiently familiar with tourists that for the popular routes you should have no problems booking. If you are out and about and have no signal then bars will call a taxi for you though you should buy something for the service.

Shared taxis

In a couple of locations, most notably Centro de Visitantes and the car park at the entrance to the Caldera de Taburiente, shared taxis operate ferrying tourists up steep routes to the start of popular walks which otherwise would be difficult or impossible to achieve.

Taxi signs & 'taxi verde'

Many villages along walks have taxi signs which can be useful, some even give prices. The 'taxi verde' scheme is along these lines for rural locations. A number is assigned to the location so tourists need only mention they are at ‘Parada Verde de Taxi’ (green taxi rank) and state the name and number of the location to be picked up from the right place.
