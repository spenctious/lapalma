About La Palma

Where is it?

La Palma is one of the western-most of the Spanish Canary Islands, part of Macronesia which includes the Azores and Madeira to the north and Cape Verde to the south. The nearest land south Morocco about 445km away. Direct flights take about four and a half hours.

What's so good about it?

Not for nothing is La Palma is known as ‘La Isla Bonita’ - the beautiful island. The greenest of the Canary Islands is only half the size of it's better known neighbour Tenerife but it has everything a nature-loving walker could ask for without any of the mass-tourism that the bigger islands have. La palma can boast:

  • A thousand kilometers of supported and maintained waymarked trails
  • One of the world's largest erosion craters
  • The last remnants of ancient laurel forest dating from the tertiary period
  • A unique string of volcanos along its spine
  • Dramatic deep-cut ravines with their own microclimates and vegetation
  • Miles of pristine coastline with surprising beachside communities
